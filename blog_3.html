Workin' hard to be chilled...

What is more important biomechanics or physilogy?

Since I was 15 I started slow walking to try and figure out my body. Figure out why it did not function, 'with ease', why it always felt under stress. I had been to see many physios to help solve a lordosis and the problems that came with it, such as years of shin splints, but the only real long term solution imo is sustained deepening understanding of one's body and its movements

In 2015 I had only just left the Army (2 Para) and my ambition was to become a yoga teacher and to improve my body functionality such that I could run on a daily basis free of muscle stress, niggles or injuries. This was the lifestyle I envisioned. In 2016 I dabbled in Muay Thai as I recovered from another long injury. Then as I recovered I got into triathlon. I had not anticipated taking it so seriously but I get to live the lifestyle I envisioned - training on a daily basis, always looking to make it more stress free than the day before. To win a race or be considered 'good' is conditional on natural physiology and in that respect I am very fortunate. To improve my performance level, it is conditional on my movement efficiency. This is where the real excitement for me lies.
