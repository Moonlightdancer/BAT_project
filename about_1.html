Workin' hard to be chilled





What is more important biomechanics or physilogy?

Since I was 15 I started slow walking to try and figure out my body. Figure out why it did not function, 'with ease', why it always felt under stress. I had been to see many physios to help solve a lordosis and the problems that came with it, such as years of shin splints, but the only real long term solution imo is sustained deepening understanding of one's body and its movements

triathlon was only meant to be a side-track from becomign a yoga teacher. I was still young and could experiment my body functionality with this sport which incorporated 3 disciplines. The idea of being able to train, stress free enough to recover for the next day while teaching yoga seemed like a great style of living. But triathlon has sucked me into its world and I've been on a whirlwind - for bad or good, I'm not sure.
